The Current System

• Nearly 1 in 4 children in the U.S. lives without a biological father in the home—yet single fathers receive less than 4% of TANF aid nationwide.

• Over 60% of families facing child protective services intervention report financial instability as a primary stressor—but no emergency financial support is offered before removal.

• Starting a business? Good luck. Average SBA microloan is $13,000, but requires a 640+ credit score, personal collateral, and a business plan judged by outdated standards.

• Mental health services have waitlists of up to 6 months in low-income areas, with 70% of counties lacking a single child psychiatrist.

• Federal aid systems rely on “head of household” forms that don’t reflect split custody, kinship care, or blended families—leaving countless caregivers invisible.

• Red tape kills support. Applying for Section 8, childcare vouchers, or vehicle repair funds can take months—and require documentation most families don’t have access to in crisis.

• Family courts incentivize conflict, not reunification. Mediation is rare. Visitation is often limited not for safety, but for lack of transportation or housing.

• Business grants often go to "innovation," not survival. There's no funding path for someone just trying to fix a van and get to work.

The Future We’re Building

• We’re creating a system where support shows up early—before families collapse, not after.

• Need-based help, not form-based. Real people with real needs—whether they check a box or not—deserve aid.

• Startup help isn’t just for tech bros. Microgrants and local investor pools will fund dignity-based businesses: car repair, childcare, mobile food shops, and more.

• Instead of punishing fathers for needing help, we empower them to lead. Equal access to TANF, emergency housing, therapy, and legal aid.

• We offer transportation assistance, family advocacy, mental health access without waiting lists, and peer mentoring in your own community.

• The Father’s Alliance is building support hubs—real-world “Help Centers” that work faster than government offices. Think garages, not offices. People, not policies.

• Courts should heal, not harm. We’ll train mediators, fund reunification, and provide courtroom advocates for parents who’ve been silenced.

• We believe your idea matters—even if it's just a pressure washer and a dream. You don’t need perfect credit to matter. You need a chance. We’re building that chance.
